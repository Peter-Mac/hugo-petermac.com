### Career Summary

I'm a solution architect with 30 years in IT, working across a wide range of industries — banking, insurance, retail, media, education, government, utilities, telco, manufacturing, and more. Most of that has been as a contractor, brought in to help organisations make sensible technology decisions and see them through to delivery.

I enjoy the architect's role because it sits where business intent meets technical reality. The interesting work is in the trade-offs — balancing cost, risk, and timeline against what the technology can actually do. I keep an eye on emerging tech because it sometimes changes those trade-offs, not because newer is automatically better.

### Key Skills and Competencies

**Architecture and solution design.** End-to-end design across a wide spectrum of industries and technology stacks. Comfortable with the full scope — picking the right components and vendors, integration design, and guiding the delivery teams that build it.

**Cross-industry experience.** Having worked across many sectors, I recognise the problem patterns that repeat — and, more importantly, the small differences that make a pattern from one industry a poor fit for another.

**Consulting and advisory.** Long track record of building trust with senior technical and business stakeholders. Comfortable translating between the two, and comfortable saying plainly when a proposed approach won't work.

**Always learning.** The technology landscape moves fast. I spend real time keeping up — reading, experimenting, and separating genuine shifts from hype — so the guidance I offer reflects where things actually are, not where they were three years ago.
